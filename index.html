About Me

I am a M.S. in Robotics (MSR) student in the Robotics Institute at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU), advised by Prof. Howie Choset at the Biorobotics Lab. I’m also working closely with Prof. Sebastian Scherer at the CMU AirLab. Before joining CMU, I received my Bachelor’s degree in Computer Science and Engineering from the Chinese University of Hong Kong, Shenzhen (CUHKSZ) in 2024, advised by Prof. Junhua Zhao.

My research interests lie in achieving robust robotic systems that enable long-term autonomous mobility and comprehensive spatial perception under challenging conditions, such as confined spaces, severe illumination changes, dynamic objects, etc. My current research focuses on Learning-based SLAM and Multi-sensor Fusion.

Outside of research, I enjoy basketball, hiking and touring, race car, LEGO, PC games, and accompanying my fiancée Waiwai! Check out my misc page for more non-research stuff :)

Research Interests: Robotics; Simultaneous Localization and Mapping; Deep Learning; Multi-sensor Fusion.
